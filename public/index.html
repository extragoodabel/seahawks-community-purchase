A Community Proposal

As directed by the will of the late, great Paul Allen, the Seahawks are to be sold, with proceeds benefiting charity. The franchise is expected to set a league record, with an estimated sale price between $7 and $10 billion.

So let’s aim higher.

We intend to place a bid of $12 billion.

Not from a billionaire.

From everyone.

Inspired by the public ownership structure of the Green Bay Packers, we propose a collective bid, raised by the Seahawks community:

One share per person.

Equal stake.

Total cost divided evenly.

No VIP class.

No majority owner.

No “control interest.”

A team for the people, by the people, of the people.

Let’s Do the Math

To own the team ourselves, we only need a mere $15,365.02 each from all 780,995 residents of Seattle. Alternatively, opening shares to all 8.1 million residents of Washington State would drop the commitment to an $1,478.72 per share. A remarkable bargain to own this historic, championship-winning team.

The more people who join, the lower the individual commitment goes.

For Perspective

Paul Allen bought the Seahawks in 1997 for roughly $194 million.

At today’s projected valuation, that represents a return north of 35 times the original investment.

Now imagine what your share might be worth in 30 years.

That is, of course, if you ever chose to sell it.

Kickoff is only -- days away. Will you be watching from your couch, or from the owner’s suite?

Join the collective bid TODAY!

The Necessary Fine Print

This proposal is conceptual. We are not collecting funds. No money is being solicited. No financial commitments are binding.

If this were ever to become real, there would be lawyers. Many lawyers.

Submitting your name does not obligate you to purchase anything. It simply signals interest in the idea of collective ownership.

That said — it would be fairly anticlimactic to enthusiastically pledge support and then disappear the moment someone hands you actual paperwork.

Email addresses are optional. If you choose to share one, it will be used solely for updates related to this project. We will not sell it, share it, trade it, lease it, or accidentally “expose it in a CSV.”

We are not affiliated with the Seattle Seahawks organization or the Paul Allen estate.

This project is an independent civic concept developed by Extra Good Studio.
